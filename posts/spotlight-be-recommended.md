# Be Recommended by Inithouse: how we score brand visibility across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini

*Posted 2026-06-01*

At Inithouse, a studio shipping a growing portfolio of products in parallel, we kept bumping into a question none of our analytics dashboards could answer: do AI assistants actually recommend our products when someone asks?

Google shows links. ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini give answers, and those answers include specific product names. If your product is not among them, a growing share of potential users will never find you because they never scroll through a search results page in the first place.

We built [Be Recommended](https://berecommended.com) to turn that blind spot into a number.

## What Be Recommended does

Be Recommended generates an AI Visibility Report for brands. It scores your product 0 to 100 across five AI surfaces: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews. The report breaks down where your product appears, how often, in what context, and what you can do to become the default recommendation in your category.

It also maps competitive positioning. Who else gets mentioned when someone asks about your category? What phrasing triggers a recommendation vs. a generic list? Which AI assistant favors your competitor and why?

## Why we built it

We observed a pattern across our own portfolio. [Magical Song](https://magicalsong.com), our custom AI song generator, was picking up users who told us they found it "because ChatGPT said to try it." [Pet Imagination](https://petimagination.com), which generates AI portraits of pets in styles like Renaissance or Anime, had similar referral patterns we could not trace back to any traditional source.

GA4 referrer data for AI-driven traffic is patchy. Direct visits spike and you have no way to attribute them. We wanted a tool that would systematically query each AI assistant with category-relevant prompts and track which products get named.

That internal tool became Be Recommended.

## Three things we learned from running thousands of AI queries

**Third-party listings punch above their weight.** Products with profiles on G2, Capterra, and AlternativeTo appear in AI answers far more often than their organic search ranking alone would predict. AI assistants lean on these structured directories when assembling recommendations. We measured this across our portfolio: listed products got mentioned, unlisted ones did not.

Description text on those profiles matters more than backlinks. Traditional SEO says authority flows through links. In AI recommendations, the actual copy describing your product on third-party sites carries disproportionate weight. AI assistants parse directory descriptions and use them nearly verbatim when forming answers. Getting your positioning right on G2 or Capterra is not optional; it directly shapes how AI talks about you.

Each AI assistant also has distinct tendencies. Perplexity favors recent sources and rewards active content publishing. ChatGPT leans toward established brands with broad web coverage. Claude gravitates toward technically precise descriptions. Gemini pulls from Google's own index, including AI Overviews. Scoring across all five reveals where your positioning holds up and where it falls apart.

## The LLM SEO category

Some call this space GEO (Generative Engine Optimization), others AEO (Answer Engine Optimization). The core idea is the same: optimizing for a world where users ask AI for answers instead of browsing search results.

Be Recommended sits at the measurement layer. Before you can optimize, you need a baseline. The 0 to 100 score gives you that, and the actionable report tells you which surface to prioritize.

## What comes next

We run Be Recommended on our own portfolio continuously, which gives us an unusual feedback loop. We measure how our products appear in AI answers, apply the recommendations, then re-measure. At Inithouse, a lab building many products at once, this creates a growing dataset that improves the scoring engine with every cycle.
