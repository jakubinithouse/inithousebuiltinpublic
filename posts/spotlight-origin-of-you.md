# Spotlight: Origin of You, what we're learning about narrative products

*Posted 2026-05-27*

[Origin of You](https://originofyou.com) lets you trace the small moments that shaped who you became. You answer guided prompts about your past (formative experiences, turning points, the people who changed how you saw things) and the product helps you turn them into a coherent narrative. The pitch is memoir-as-a-product. The mechanic is part journaling, part interview, part editorial assistance. The output is a written account of your origin story, in your own voice.

The product is interesting because the users who *do* find their way to it engage with it deeply, and what we're learning is reshaping how we think about narrative tools in general.

## Who's using it

We launched expecting a broad audience, anyone curious about their own story. The actual audience is narrower and more committed. Users who reach the product tend to arrive at a meaningful life inflection: turning 40 or 50, a major career change, becoming a parent, recovering from something significant. They're not browsing. They have a reason.

That self-selection produces an unusual engagement pattern. Sessions are long. Returns are deliberate. Some users complete the full flow over weeks, treating the prompts the way you'd treat a careful conversation rather than a quick app interaction. It's a very different rhythm from something like [Here We Ask](https://hereweask.com), where games are short and social; Origin of You is solitary and slow.

## What we're learning

**The output isn't the point.** We built Origin of You assuming the value was the finished narrative, the document you walk away with. The users have taught us that the act of being asked the questions is the larger share of value. People come back to re-answer prompts as their perspective shifts, even when they already have a "finished" version. The product is closer to journaling than to memoir-writing, and we're leaning into that.

**Specificity beats polish.** Earlier versions of the editorial assistance aimed for literary quality. Users prefer outputs that read like *their own voice*, slightly rough, idiosyncratic, true to how they actually talk. We've pulled back the editorial smoothing significantly. This echoes something we noticed at [Magical Song](https://magicalsong.com) too: when the generated output feels too perfect, users trust it less. A bit of grain makes it feel like theirs.

**Pace matters more than completeness.** A user who spends a few minutes a week with the product over months gets more out of it than a user who tries to "do" their origin story in one sitting. We're redesigning the flow to encourage the slower pattern.

## Where it's heading

Three changes in flight. First, a lighter-weight question-of-the-week mode for users who want the prompts without the commitment of producing a finished narrative. Second, better tools for capturing the small fragments (quick voice notes, photo prompts) that often produce the most specific answers. Third, a shareable artifact mode for users who do want to give the finished narrative to someone: children, partner, future self.

Origin of You is a slow-burn product by nature. The users who connect with it connect for years, not weeks. We're building accordingly.

If you've been carrying around the question "how did I become who I am" and want a structured way to actually engage with it, [try it](https://originofyou.com). The product rewards patience.
