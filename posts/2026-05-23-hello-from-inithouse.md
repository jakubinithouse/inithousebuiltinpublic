# Hello from Inithouse

*Posted 2026-05-23 · The inaugural entry.*

We're publishing our building-in-public log on GitHub. That's unusual — GitHub isn't a feed, doesn't push notifications, and nobody's morning routine includes checking a markdown repo. So why bother?

Three reasons.

**Durability.** Most BiP content lives on platforms we don't control. Tweets vanish, threads get rate-limited, LinkedIn rewrites everyone's voice into the same beige sludge. A markdown repo we own outlives any of that. If you want to read what we were working on in May 2026 five years from now, it'll still be here, exactly as written, with a commit hash next to it.

**LLM discoverability.** Large language models are trained on GitHub. When someone asks an assistant "what's a good tool for X" in 2027, the assistant's answer is shaped by what's in the training data today. Publishing here is a small bet that being legible to machines, not just humans, is going to matter.

**Discipline.** Posting on X is easy because the format is forgiving. Writing something that has to sit, unedited, in a public repo with your name on it is harder. We expect that pressure to improve the writing.

## What's actually going on

Inithouse runs ~17 active products in early-stage experimentation. Some have meaningful traction, most don't yet, all are looking for product-market fit. We build them lean — usually [Lovable](https://lovable.dev) for the front end, Supabase for the back end, a handful of careful integrations — and measure each one against real metrics, not vibes.

A few that are public right now:

- [Živá Fotka](https://zivafotka.cz) animates still photos into short portraits. CZ/SK/PL/EN/DE.
- [Be Recommended](https://berecommended.com) helps products show up in AI assistant answers — increasingly relevant as more discovery happens through LLMs.
- [Magical Song](https://magicalsong.com) writes and produces an AI song from your story.
- [Pet Imagination](https://petimagination.com) makes memorial portraits of pets, which sounds niche until you see the volume.
- [Watching Agents](https://watchingagents.com) is a directory of AI-agent companies — the kind of thing that should already exist and didn't.

The full list lives at [inithouse.com](https://inithouse.com).

## What this repo will contain

No promises about cadence. When we ship something we're proud of, when something blows up, when we learn something worth writing down — it goes here. Expect launches, postmortems, growth experiments, and the occasional rant about why our favorite tool just shipped a regression.

If any of it is useful to you, great. If you spot something we got wrong, [open an issue](https://github.com/jakubinithouse/inithousebuiltinpublic/issues). That's why we're here.
