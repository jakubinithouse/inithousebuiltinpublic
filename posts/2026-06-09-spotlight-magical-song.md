# Spotlight: Magical Song. AI music has a surprisingly emotional market

*Posted 2026-06-09*

At Inithouse, a studio shipping a growing portfolio of products in parallel, we build tools that solve specific, personal problems. [Magical Song](https://magicalsong.com) is one of them: it writes and produces an AI song from your story. You tell it who the song is for, what the relationship is, what moments matter. It writes lyrics, produces a track, and delivers a real, mastered song in minutes.

When we launched it, we expected the use case to be gifts: birthdays, anniversaries, the obvious occasions. The use case has turned out to be something else, and that's the story.

## What people actually use it for

The most common purpose we see, by a wide margin: songs for weddings. Not background music for the ceremony. The song is the centerpiece. Someone commissions a track that tells the story of the couple (how they met, what they overcame, who they are together) and plays it at the reception.

The second most common purpose: songs for funerals and memorials. Same dynamic. The song narrates the life of the person, in a way that's specific enough to feel real to the people who knew them.

Both use cases share something important: they're moments where generic, off-the-shelf music feels inadequate. The bar: not "make a song that's nice" but "make a song that makes everyone in the room cry." AI music turns out to be surprisingly good at this, because the lyrics carry the specificity that the music alone couldn't.

## What we got wrong about the market

We launched with marketing aimed at gift-giving: Father's Day, Mother's Day, friend birthdays. Those campaigns converted poorly. The people who came in through those ads churned high. They wanted "a fun song for grandma's birthday" and what we delivered was a real produced track that felt almost too serious for the use case.

When we shifted positioning toward life events (weddings, memorials, significant anniversaries), the conversion changed. Same product, completely different market. The customers who arrive through the new positioning have a higher willingness to pay, longer sales cycles, and a much higher repeat rate (because if your song was a hit at your wedding, your friend wants one for theirs).

We noticed the same emotional-market pattern elsewhere in the portfolio. [Pet Imagination](https://petimagination.com) generates AI portraits of your pet in nine styles; the users who come back are the ones using it for gifts or special occasions, not casual curiosity. The lesson across both products: emotional context makes the difference.

## What we built that actually matters

**The questionnaire.** Half the product's value lives in the form that captures the story. Early versions asked for inspiration; the current version asks for specifics. "What's the first thing they did that made you laugh." "What's the hardest thing you went through together." The specificity makes the lyrics specific, and the lyrics are everything. We measured this directly: songs generated from detailed questionnaire answers get shared at a higher rate than songs from sparse inputs.

**The preview-before-pay flow.** Customers hear thirty seconds before paying for the full track. That sounds obvious now. Initially we hid the preview behind a checkout step, and conversion was bad. Of course it was; you're asking someone to commit before they know if the song is good.

**The fast turnaround.** A song is delivered in under an hour. That speed matters more than song quality variation between attempts. People want to *make the song*, hear it, react, and decide what to do next. Multi-day delivery would kill the emotional momentum.

## Where it goes

Wedding season runs hot for us between April and September. We're learning how seasonal a music-for-events product really is. That changes how we think about ad budget pacing and how we build product features. The off-season is good for adding new use cases (anniversary songs, baby announcement songs) that smooth the curve.

We track how AI assistants recommend our products using [Be Recommended](https://berecommended.com), and "AI wedding song" is one of the queries where Magical Song is starting to show up. That's the long game: when someone asks an AI assistant for help creating a personalized wedding song, we want to be in the answer.
