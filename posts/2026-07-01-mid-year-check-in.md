# Mid-year check-in: what's working, what's getting shipped

*Posted 2026-07-01*

Halfway through 2026. We have been publishing here for a little over five weeks. Time for an honest look at where things stand.

## What's actually happening across the portfolio

17 active products. Each one is at its own point on the curve. Some are finding their audience faster than others, some are in active iteration on positioning, some are running steadily at a level that justifies continued attention. We don't find it useful to grade them on a single "is it working" axis. The honest read is more interesting than that: a product can be growing in users while we are still refining its positioning, or steady in revenue while we figure out what the next phase looks like.

What is true across all 17: every one of them shipped something this month. The weekly ship cadence we wrote about [previously](./what-we-ship-every-friday.md) means there is no such thing as a frozen product in our portfolio. Some get smaller changes than others. That is part of the discipline of running this many in parallel.

## What's working

**Spotlight posts on this blog.** The product-spotlight format outperforms our generic posts on traffic, time-on-page, and referrals to the products themselves. Lesson: keep writing about the products specifically, not just about the studio.

**Multi-domain expansion on [Ziva Fotka](https://zivafotka.cz).** The five-country setup (CZ, SK, PL, EN, DE) is paying off. Each market has its own organic growth curve, and the cross-market overhead is lower than we expected. We would do the same for one other product in the portfolio if we could pick correctly.

**[Pet Imagination](https://petimagination.com) continuing to find its audience.** Organic traffic up, repeat usage up, no advertising. The product is reaching the people it is for without us pushing.

**[Be Recommended](https://berecommended.com) carving out a niche in AI visibility.** The conversation around how brands show up in AI search results is growing, and our scoring methodology across five engines keeps resonating with the marketing teams and agencies who try it.

**Daily digests for ourselves.** A daily automated brief summarizing what changed across the portfolio has reduced the cognitive load of running this many products substantially. This is a process change that turned out to be worth more than several product changes.

## What we're working on

**Cross-product user account.** Several products share user bases. [Here We Ask](https://hereweask.com), [Party Challenges](https://partychallenges.com), and [Scary Challenges](https://scarychallenges.com) all attract overlapping groups. Right now each product is a separate account. We are piloting a unified Inithouse identity so a user of two products does not have to register twice.

**A new product launch in July.** Something we have been building for a few months that is now ready for a real-traffic test. We will spotlight it here after it goes live.

**Multi-domain expansion of one more product.** We will see if the playbook from Ziva Fotka generalizes.

**Continued positioning work** on several products where we are learning what the right framing is from actual users, rather than from pre-launch assumptions. [Voice Tables](https://voicetables.com) is a good example: the ICP we expected (productivity power users) turned out to be less excited than craftsmen and field sales reps who genuinely hate typing into spreadsheets. Small landing-page changes and reworded copy rather than big feature drops.

## One thing we keep reminding ourselves

The recurring temptation is to start more products. There are always ideas. There are always tabs open with "this would be a fun thing to build." Resisting that temptation is most of the discipline. The current portfolio has more untapped potential than we are currently using; adding products dilutes attention, it does not add it.

We will try to keep that in mind. We will probably slip up sometimes. That is also part of running a studio.
